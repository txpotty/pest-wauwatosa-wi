Signs You Might Need Squirrel Removal in Wauwatosa, WI

Recognizing the signs of a squirrel infestation early on is key to minimizing damage and the cost of removal. If you've observed any of the following, it's a strong indicator that you may need professional squirrel removal services from Wauwatosa Pest Pros:

Noises in the Attic or Walls

One of the most common signs of squirrels in your home is the sounds they make. Listen for scratching, scurrying, chewing, or even chirping sounds coming from your attic, walls, or ceilings, especially during the day. Squirrels are most active during daylight hours as they forage for food and build nests. These noises can be unsettling and disrupt your peace within your own home environment. Unlike rodents that are more active at night, squirrels are diurnal, meaning they are active in the morning and evening. The sounds they make can be quite distinct, often louder than mice or rats due to their larger size. If you hear these sounds, it's a good idea to investigate or call a professional to confirm the source. These noises can also be a tell-tale sign of other wildlife issues such as raccoons or even birds, highlighting the importance of a proper assessment.

Chewed Wires and Insulation

Squirrels have a constant need to chew to keep their teeth sharp. Unfortunately, the materials they often chew on when in your home are incredibly destructive. Electrical wires are a common target, and chewing on them creates a significant fire hazard. They also chew on insulation, wood beams, and ductwork, causing structural damage and impacting your home's energy efficiency. Finding pieces of chewed material or exposed wiring are clear indicators of squirrel activity. The damage can range from minor nicks on wires to complete destruction of insulation in certain areas. This can lead to increased heating and cooling costs and, more importantly, create dangerous conditions within your home. Addressing chewed wires is paramount for safety. Professionals can not only remove the squirrels but also assess and repair electrical damage, preventing potential catastrophic events.

Visible Entry Points

Squirrels are athletic and can squeeze through surprisingly small openings. Look for gaps or holes in your roofline, damaged soffits or fascia, openings around chimneys or vents, and even small cracks in your foundation. Squirrels are excellent climbers and use these entry points to access your attic or walls. These openings might be obvious or hidden from casual view. Inspecting your home's exterior, especially at higher levels, is crucial for identifying these vulnerabilities. Even small gaps of an inch or two can be large enough for a squirrel to enter. Trees with branches touching your roof can also provide easy access for squirrels. Identifying and sealing these entry points is a vital step in preventing future squirrel problems. A professional inspection will thoroughly check all potential access points, even those you might not consider.

Squirrel Droppings and Urine

Finding squirrel feces or a strong, unpleasant odor in areas like your attic or garage is a sure sign of an infestation. Squirrel droppings are typically pellet-shaped and about the size of a small bean. Their urine can soak into insulation and wood, creating a persistent odor and contributing to potential health issues. The accumulation of droppings and urine can also attract other pests and create unsanitary conditions. The odor can be musty or ammonia-like and become stronger over time. It's important to not handle squirrel droppings without proper protection as they can carry diseases. Professional cleaning and sanitization are recommended to safely remove contaminated materials and eliminate odors. This is similar to the concerns you might have with rodent control, where droppings also pose health risks.

Seeing Squirrels Entering or Leaving Your Home

Perhaps the most obvious sign is directly observing squirrels entering or exiting your home through a specific opening. If you see a squirrel repeatedly using a particular spot to access your attic or roof, it's a clear indication they have made your home a part of their territory. Observing this behavior can also help pinpoint the main entry point, making the removal process more efficient. You might see them carrying nesting materials like leaves or twigs into the opening. This confirms they are setting up residence inside your home. While spotting a squirrel on your roof or in your yard is normal, observing them consistently entering your structure is a cause for concern and warrants immediate action to prevent further infestation and damage.

Nesting Material Accumulation

Squirrels build nests using materials like leaves, twigs, insulation, shredded paper, and fabric. Finding piles of these materials in your attic, walls, or chimney is a strong indicator of a squirrel nest. These nests are often bulky and can be found in secluded, protected areas within your home's structure. The presence of nesting material confirms that the squirrels are not just passing through but are actively living and potentially breeding within your home. These nests can also harbor other pests, adding to the problem. Removing the nesting material is an important part of the cleanup process after the squirrels have been removed. It's important to be cautious when exploring areas where you suspect nesting, as the squirrels may be defensive of their young. Professional removal ensures that the nest is safely and completely removed.

Aggressive Behavior of Squirrels

While squirrels are generally timid, those that have become accustomed to living near or in human structures can sometimes exhibit bolder or even aggressive behavior, especially if they feel threatened or are protecting a nest. If you notice squirrels acting unusually bold, approaching you or pets without hesitation, or displaying defensive postures, it could be a sign they are comfortable and established in or around your home. This is not a definitive sign of an internal infestation, but it does indicate a higher level of interaction with squirrels in your immediate environment. It can also be a sign they are used to being fed by humans, which can lead to them becoming a nuisance. While rare, a cornered squirrel can bite, so it's best to maintain a safe distance and let professionals handle removal.

Sudden Increase in Squirrel Population

If you notice a sudden and significant increase in the number of squirrels around your property, it could indicate that your home is providing a favorable habitat for them, perhaps due to accessible food sources or shelter within your structure. While a general increase in wildlife in your area could be due to environmental factors, a localized surge in squirrel activity focusing on your property might mean your home has become a breeding ground or a reliable food source. This could also be linked to nearby construction or changes in their natural habitat that are driving them towards residential areas like Brookfield or Elm Grove. An increase in sightings, especially of younger squirrels, could point to a nest being established within your home.

Understanding Squirrel Behavior and Biology

To effectively deal with squirrel infestations, it's helpful to understand their natural behavior and biology. Squirrels are part of the rodent family and are known for their agility, climbing skills, and ability to store food for the winter. There are several species of squirrels found in Wisconsin, with the most common being the gray squirrel. Understanding their habits can shed light on why they might choose your home as a nesting site and how to better deter them.

Squirrel Diet and Foraging

Squirrels primarily feed on nuts, seeds, fruits, and acorns. They are avid hoarders, burying nuts and seeds in numerous locations for later retrieval. This foraging behavior often brings them into residential areas where bird feeders, fruit trees, and garden vegetables provide easy meals. Their constant search for food is a primary driver of their activity and can sometimes lead them to explore potential food sources within or around your home, like stored pet food or garbage cans. Understanding their diet helps us identify potential attractants on your property that might be drawing them in. Removing these food sources or securing them properly can be a proactive step in prevention.

Nesting Habits

Squirrels build nests for shelter and to raise their young. They typically have two breeding seasons each year, one in late winter/early spring and another in late summer/early autumn. Their nests, called dreys, are often constructed in tree cavities or on branches using leaves and twigs. However, when natural nesting sites are limited or weather conditions are harsh, your attic, chimney, or wall voids can become attractive alternatives. These protected spaces offer warmth and safety from predators and the elements. Female squirrels are particularly drawn to these areas when they are ready to give birth and raise their young. Identifying and removing these nests after the squirrels are gone is crucial as they can contain parasites and diseases.

Squirrel Agility and Climbing

Squirrels are impressive climbers and can navigate vertical surfaces with ease. Their sharp claws and strong legs allow them to climb trees, walls, fences, and even rough siding. This agility is what enables them to access elevated entry points on your home, such as roofs and attic vents. Overhanging tree branches are often used as a bridge to reach your house. Keeping tree branches trimmed away from your roofline is a simple yet effective preventative measure against squirrels. Their ability to jump considerable distances also contributes to their access to difficult-to-reach areas on your property.

Communication and Social Behavior

Squirrels communicate through vocalizations, tail movements, and scent marking. They are generally not highly social animals but can be seen interacting with each other during mating seasons or when competing for resources. Their territorial nature can sometimes lead to aggression towards other squirrels or even pets. Understanding their communication can sometimes help in identifying their presence, such as alarm calls when they feel threatened. While generally wary of humans, repeated positive interactions, such as being fed, can lead to them becoming less fearful and more likely to approach your home.

Understanding these aspects of squirrel behavior helps Wauwatosa Pest Pros develop effective and humane removal strategies. We tailor our approach based on the specific species of squirrel and their observed behaviors on your property, ensuring the best possible outcome for squirrel removal in Wauwatosa, WI. We also look at the broader picture, considering what might be attracting them to your property in the first place, providing advice on habitat modification and deterrents.

Preventing Future Squirrel Infestations

Removing squirrels is the immediate step, but preventing their return is equally important for long-term peace of mind. Wauwatosa Pest Pros can help you implement effective strategies to make your home less appealing to squirrels and seal off potential access points. We offer follow-up services and advice to help you maintain a squirrel-free home. Our preventative measures are designed to be both effective and environmentally conscious.

Trim Overhanging Branches

As mentioned earlier, tree branches touching your roof or siding are a common highway for squirrels to access your home. Regularly trimming these branches back at least 6-8 feet from your house significantly reduces their ability to jump onto your roof. This is a simple yet highly effective deterrent. Consider the types of trees on your property and their growth patterns when planning your trimming schedule. Certain tree species are more attractive to squirrels due to their nuts or fruits, making regular trimming even more important.

Secure Potential Entry Points

Conduct a thorough inspection of your home's exterior and seal any gaps, cracks, or openings larger than about an inch. Pay close attention to areas around vents, chimneys, windows, and doors. Use durable materials like hardware cloth (½ inch mesh or smaller) to cover vent openings and seal larger gaps with caulk or expanding foam designed for exterior use. Ensure chimney caps are securely in place. Regularly inspect these sealed areas for any new damage or signs of tampering by squirrels or other wildlife. This is a continuous process as your home settles and weather can cause minor damage over time.

Protect Food Sources

Remove or secure anything that might attract squirrels to your yard. Use squirrel-proof bird feeders, clean up fallen nuts and fruits from trees, and store garbage in secure bins with tight-fitting lids. Avoid leaving pet food outdoors overnight. Consider fencing your garden to protect vegetables and fruits. Even small food scraps can attract squirrels, so practicing good sanitation around your property is beneficial. If you enjoy feeding birds, consider placing feeders away from your house or using feeders specifically designed to deter squirrels. Squirrels are intelligent and will quickly exploit any easily accessible food source.

Install Chimney Caps and Vent Covers

Uncapped chimneys and open attic vents are inviting entry points for squirrels looking for shelter. Installing sturdy chimney caps and vent covers made of metal mesh will prevent squirrels and other animals from entering these areas. Ensure the mesh size is small enough to exclude squirrels but still allows for proper ventilation. Regularly check these covers to ensure they are secure and undamaged. These are often overlooked entry points but can be major access points for wildlife looking for a warm, dry place to nest, particularly during colder months here in Wauwatosa, WI, and nearby Greenfield.

Consider Habitat Modification

Evaluate the environment around your home. Are there dense bushes or shrubs that squirrels could use for cover near your house? Consider thinning out dense vegetation close to your foundation or siding. While you don't want to eliminate all natural habitat, making the immediate area around your home less appealing to squirrels can help. This is a long-term strategy that complements the immediate removal and exclusion steps. Think about the entire ecosystem around your property and how it might be attracting unwelcome visitors. Consulting with a landscaping professional could also provide insight into plant choices that are less attractive to squirrels.

By implementing these preventative measures in conjunction with professional squirrel removal in Wauwatosa, WI from Wauwatosa Pest Pros, you can significantly reduce the likelihood of future infestations and enjoy a squirrel-free home environment. Our team can provide further advice and assistance with these preventative strategies tailored to your specific property and the surrounding environment.
